You are building a minimal and fast public-transit app for Israel that replaces Moovit/BusNearby behavior but fixes reliability gaps.
Speak to users. No tech jargon. Focus on UX, clarity, and speed.

Core flow
1) Home screen: two fields and a big button.
   - From: use current location or type a place.
   - To: type a place.
   - Button: “Find routes”.
   - Small note: “Beta • delay‑aware suggestions”.

2) Results list:
   - Show 3–6 best options. Each row shows total time in minutes, # of transfers, earliest arrival, and a simple line of the legs (WALK → BUS → TRAIN → BUS).
   - Add a tiny “reliability bar” that accounts for late buses. Low reliability = warn the user.
   - Mark risky 1‑minute transfers as “tight” and suggest safer alternatives with +3–8 min buffer.

3) Route details:
   - Map on top with a clean polyline. Do not overload with icons.
   - Below the map: step list with times. Emphasize transfer points and how much buffer you have.
   - Show a small “Why this route?” that explains tradeoffs in plain language.

4) Live navigation:
   - While moving, show the next stop and countdown. If traffic worsens, suggest “Get off earlier at ___ and switch to ___” when it is truly better.
   - Silent by default. One crisp alert before each transfer and before alighting.

Design
- Minimal, bright, Hebrew and English friendly. Large type. Clear contrasts. No clutter.
- Map is the hero. Keep other UI elements slim.
- Always accessible: color-safe, big tap targets, RTL support.

Extra niceties
- Favorite places: Home, Work, Custom.
- “Safer transfers” toggle to avoid stressful 1–2 minute switches.
- “Avoid delayed corridors” hint when a bus line is late at this hour.

Connect to backend
- Load the provided `openapi.json` into your tool and generate the client.
- Use `/v1/routes/search` for options. Send from/to coordinates and optional notes.
- Use `/v1/trip/start` and `/v1/trip/progress` to start and track a session.
- Show `/health/` on a small debug page to verify connectivity.
